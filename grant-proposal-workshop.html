Lesson Aims

By the end of this lesson, you will be able to...

  • Analyze and compare two different grant proposal templates.
  • Use the second conditional to discuss hypothetical situations and justify decisions.
  • Use connected speech to improve pronunciation and fluency.
  • Collaborate with a team to choose a funding strategy and present your reasoning.

Activity: Main Task - Choosing a Grant Proposal

You are a team in a new NGO. In your breakout room, analyze the grant options, decide on the best one, and prepare your justification.

Scenario:

You are part of a team at a new youth-led NGO in Nablus. Your project provides digital literacy training for university graduates to help them find freelance work. You need to secure funding.

Your Task (10 minutes in breakout rooms):

  1. Access the documents: Your teacher will post a link to a Google Doc in the chat.
  2. Analyze: Use the comparison table in the document to analyze the pros and cons of Template A (Al-Quds Innovation Fund) and Template B (Digital Futures Foundation).
  3. Decide: As a group, choose the template that is a better fit for your project.
  4. Prepare: Prepare a 2-minute justification for your choice. You must use the second conditional. For example: "We chose Template A. If we used Template B, we would have to..."

Google Doc: Comparison Table

Use this table to analyze the two funding options.

Feature Template A: Al-Quds Innovation Fund (Local) Template B: Digital Futures Foundation (International)
Funding Amount Up to $10,000 Up to $50,000
Application Deadline In 2 weeks In 3 months
Reporting Requirements Short quarterly reports Detailed monthly reports with financial audits
Focus Area Must show direct benefit to the local Nablus economy Must align with global digital skills trends

Activity: Justify & Discuss

You will be paired with another group. Take turns presenting and listening. Your goal is collaborative discussion, not debate.

Group 1: Present for 2 minutes.

Group 2: Listen for strengths.

  • Find one thing you agree with in their reasoning.
  • Use these prompts to start a discussion:
    • "Tell me more about..." (e.g., ...why you weren't worried about the reporting.)
    • "I wonder if..." (e.g., ...if you had more time, you would make the same decision.)

Then, swap roles.

Contingency Plan:

If you finish early, discuss: What would be the biggest challenge in implementing the project with the funding you chose?
